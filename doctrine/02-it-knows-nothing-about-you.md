# Rule 2: Right Now, Your AI Knows Nothing About You

## The Problem

Open ChatGPT or Claude right now. It has no idea:

- What your specialty is
- What procedures you perform
- How many years you've been in practice
- What your documentation standards look like
- Whether you're an attending, a fellow, or a resident

It's talking to you the same way it talks to a patient Googling symptoms at 2 AM. Of course the output is generic — you never told it you're a physician.

## The Fix

Every AI tool lets you store background information about yourself. This runs silently behind every conversation. Once you set it up, every single output improves automatically.

**In ChatGPT:** Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions
**In Claude:** Settings → Profile → Custom Instructions

You fill this in once. It takes 15 minutes. Then it works forever.

## What To Put In There

Here's what your AI needs to know about you. Use what fits:

**Who you are:**
- Your specialty and subspecialty
- How many years you've been in practice
- What procedures you perform regularly
- Whether you teach, do research, or hold administrative roles

**What good work looks like:**
- Your documentation style (concise vs. detailed, structured vs. narrative)
- Your preferred evidence sources (ACC/AHA, ESC, SCAI, specific journals)
- What your audience needs (referring physicians, patients, committees, trainees)

**What to avoid:**
- Patient-facing disclaimers ("consult your doctor") — you are the doctor
- Generic medical advice that could come from WebMD
- Hedging every statement into uselessness

## Before vs. After

**Before setup (default AI):**
> You: "Help me think through this complex PCI case."
> AI: "Sure! Percutaneous coronary intervention is a procedure used to open blocked arteries. Before proceeding, it's important to consider the patient's overall health, the location of the blockage, and potential risks. You should consult with your care team..."

**After setup (configured AI):**
> You: "Help me think through this complex PCI case."
> AI: "What's the anatomy? I'll help you think through access strategy, lesion prep, stent sizing, and whether you need hemodynamic support. Are we talking about a CTO, heavy calcium, bifurcation, or left main? And what's the LV function — that changes whether you want Impella standby."

Same question. Wildly different output. The only thing that changed is that the AI knows who it's talking to.

## The One-Sentence Rule

**The more your AI knows about you and your practice, the less time you spend fixing its output.**

Set it up once. Benefit forever.
