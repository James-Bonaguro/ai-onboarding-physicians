# Rule 3: Talk To It Like You're Briefing a New Fellow

## The Mistake

People Google "best AI prompts" and copy-paste them like magic spells:

> "Act as a world-class cardiologist with 30 years of experience at the Mayo Clinic who specializes in..."

This is useless. The AI doesn't become smarter because you told it to pretend to be someone impressive.

## What Actually Works

Talk to it the way you'd brief a sharp first-year fellow on their first day in the cath lab.

You wouldn't hand a fellow a magic phrase and walk away. You'd say:

> "Here's the patient. Here's what I need you to look at. Here's what matters. Here's what to watch out for. Go."

That's it. That's the entire skill.

## The Formula

Every good AI conversation follows four steps:

| Step | What it means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **What I have** | Give it the raw material — the data, the scenario, the context | "72-year-old male, NSTEMI, troponin peaked at 12, EF 40%, cath showed 3-vessel disease with a critical LAD lesion." |
| **What I need** | Tell it exactly what to produce | "I need to think through revascularization strategy — PCI vs. CABG — given this anatomy and function." |
| **What to avoid** | Set guardrails so it doesn't waste your time | "Don't give me a textbook overview of revascularization. Focus on what the SYNTAX score and FREEDOM trial data say about this specific scenario." |
| **What to fix** | After the first draft, tell it what to change | "You're overweighting the surgical option. Factor in that this patient has a hostile chest from prior radiation. Now what?" |

## Real Example

**Bad (copy-pasted prompt):**
> "Act as a senior cardiologist and help me with this patient."

**Good (briefing a fellow):**
> "I have a 65-year-old diabetic woman with stable angina, positive stress test showing moderate anterior ischemia, and a cath showing 80% mid-LAD with heavy calcium. EF is 55%. I'm thinking rotational atherectomy and DES. Walk me through whether the evidence supports PCI here versus medical management alone, given ISCHEMIA trial data. Then help me think through lesion prep strategy if we go ahead."

The second version works because you told it what you have, what you need, what to focus on, and how to think about it. That's not "prompt engineering." That's just clear communication.

## The One-Sentence Rule

**Don't search for the perfect prompt. Just tell it what you'd tell a smart person sitting next to you.**
