# Rule 4: Your First Answer Is a First Draft, Not a Final Product

## What Most People Do

They type something into AI. They get a response. They think: "This is okay but not great." Then they close the window and go do it themselves.

That's like asking a fellow to write a procedure note, reading the first draft, and doing it yourself instead of giving them feedback.

## What To Do Instead

The first response is never the final product. It's a starting point. The real skill is knowing what to say next.

After every AI response, you have three moves:

| Move | When to use it | What to say |
|---|---|---|
| **Sharpen** | The direction is right but it's too vague or generic | "This is too high-level. I need specific trial data and numbers, not general statements." |
| **Redirect** | It went in the wrong direction | "You're focused on the procedural approach but I need help with the post-procedure anticoagulation plan. Start over with that focus." |
| **Push deeper** | The surface answer is fine but you need more | "Good start. Now what happens if this patient also has HIT history? How does that change the anticoagulation strategy?" |

## Real Example

**Round 1 — You:**
> "Draft a procedure note for a diagnostic left heart cath with PCI to the mid-LAD."

**Round 1 — AI:**
> Gives you a general procedure note template.

**Round 2 — You:**
> "The findings section is too thin. I used rotational atherectomy with a 1.5mm burr, predilated with a 2.5mm NC balloon, and placed a 3.0 x 38mm DES. TIMI 3 flow at the end. Add those details and include the fluoroscopy time, contrast volume, and access site closure method."

**Round 2 — AI:**
> Now gives you a specific, detailed procedure note with all the elements.

**Round 3 — You:**
> "Good. Now make sure the indication section clearly supports medical necessity for billing. The patient had positive stress test, refractory angina on max medical therapy, and a de novo lesion. That needs to be explicit."

**Round 3 — AI:**
> Gives you a note that's structured for both clinical documentation and billing compliance.

Three rounds. Maybe 5 minutes total. You went from a generic template to a usable procedure note.

## The Skill

The people who get the most out of AI aren't better at writing the first message. They're better at the second and third message. They know how to steer.

Most people quit after Round 1. That's like reading the first draft of anything and expecting it to be perfect.

## The One-Sentence Rule

**Never accept the first response as the final product. The real value is in rounds 2 and 3.**
