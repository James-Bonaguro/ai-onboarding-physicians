# AI for Physicians — Setup Guide

## What This Is

Every AI tool you're using right now is running on default settings. It doesn't know you're a physician. It doesn't know your specialty, your documentation standards, or how you think through clinical problems. That's why the outputs feel like they were written for a medical student. This fixes that.

## Before You Start

**Read this first:** [Safe Use — HIPAA and Patient Data](setup/02-safe-use.md). You need to know the boundaries before you start using AI with anything clinical. The short version: never put patient identifiers into consumer AI tools. Everything else is explained in that guide.

## How This Works

**1. Five Rules** — Read these once. They take 10 minutes. They change how you use AI forever.

**2. Setup** — Copy-paste instructions to configure your AI so it knows you're a physician. Takes 15 minutes. You do this once.

**3. Try It** — Your first week of actually using AI for real work. Fifteen minutes a day for five days.

Total investment: about two hours across a week. After that, you'll know whether this is useful for your practice or not.

## What Changes After Setup

- Clinical questions get answered with specific trial data and guideline citations — not WebMD summaries
- Documentation drafts come back in your note format, not generic templates
- AI challenges your clinical reasoning instead of agreeing with everything you say
- Administrative work — prior auth letters, referral responses — takes minutes instead of hours

## What AI Cannot Do

- It cannot access your EHR, your patients' data, or real-time clinical information
- It is not a diagnostic tool and does not replace clinical judgment
- It has a training data cutoff — always verify guideline currency against primary sources
- It can hallucinate references — verify citations for anything you're publishing or presenting
- It is a thinking partner and a drafting tool, not an oracle
